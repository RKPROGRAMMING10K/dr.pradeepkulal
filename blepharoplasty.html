Blepharoplasty

Blepharoplasty is a surgical procedure that is commonly referred to as eyelid surgery. It is a cosmetic surgical procedure that is done to improve the appearance of the eyelids. The procedure involves the removal of excess skin, muscle, and sometimes fat from the upper and/or lower eyelids.

The primary goal of blepharoplasty is to address sagging or drooping eyelids that can give a person a tired or older appearance. The procedure can also improve vision in some cases where sagging eyelids are obstructing a person's line of sight.

Blepharoplasty can be done as a standalone procedure or in conjunction with other cosmetic procedures such as a facelift or brow lift. The surgery is typically performed on an outpatient basis and recovery time varies depending on the extent of the surgery.

It is important to note that while blepharoplasty can improve the appearance of the eyelids, it cannot address other signs of aging such as wrinkles or dark circles around the eyes.

Facelifts

Facelift surgery, also known as rhytidectomy, is a cosmetic surgical procedure that is done to improve the appearance of the face by removing wrinkles and sagging skin. It is typically performed on individuals who are looking to reduce the signs of aging and restore a more youthful appearance to their face.

During a facelift, the surgeon makes incisions around the ears and possibly in the hairline to lift and reposition the underlying facial tissues. Excess skin is removed and the remaining skin is pulled tight and sutured into place. The procedure can be performed on the face, neck, and sometimes the brow area.

Facelift surgery can also be combined with other cosmetic procedures such as eyelid surgery, brow lift, or neck lift for a more comprehensive rejuvenation of the face.

Recovery time after a facelift varies depending on the extent of the surgery, but generally, patients are advised to rest and avoid strenuous activities for several weeks. Bruising and swelling are common after the surgery, but these will gradually subside over time.

It is important to note that while facelift surgery can improve the appearance of the face, it cannot stop the aging process altogether. Maintenance procedures such as non-surgical treatments like injectables, laser treatments, or chemical peels may be necessary to maintain the results of the surgery over time.
